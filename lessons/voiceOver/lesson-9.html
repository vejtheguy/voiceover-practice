Lesson 9: Understanding the Rotor Tool (Part 1)

The Rotor is a customizable menu that pops up on your screen. Instead of tapping a button to open it, you activate it with a twisting gesture.

Using the Rotor

Think of the Rotor like an invisible physical dial or knob sitting on your screen. To use it, rotate two fingers on the screen just like you are turning a dial or twisting a cap off a water bottle.

Tip: Put two fingers on the screen (like your thumb and index finger). Leave a little space between them, and twist them clockwise or counterclockwise. A visual will pop up on the screen allowing you to view the rotor selection items.

Note: Twisting clockwise rotates down through your custom menu list from the top. Twisting counterclockwise moves up through the menu list from the bottom.

Practice: Rotate Rotor

Try practicing rotating through the rotor tool now. It takes some time to get used to the motion!

Note: You can lift your two fingers and readjust your hand position while rotating.

Customizing your Rotor Tool

You can go into your settings to change what items appear in the rotor selection list.

My favorite options to add to the rotor tool as a beginner:

  • Speech Rate (changes the text-to-speech speed)
  • Language (changes the language of the text-to-speech)
