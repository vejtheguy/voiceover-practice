Lesson 2: Mute and Pause

If you would like to mute and pause VoiceOver from speaking, use two fingers and a single tap anywhere on the screen. If you would like to unmute and resume VoiceOver, use the same two finger single tap gesture again VoiceOver will continue reading from where it left off.

Task: Try Muting

With VoiceOver on, single tap the text below. While the text is being read, practice muting and unmuting the screen reader.

This is a bunch of text I have put here for practicing. At any point, once you are annoyed by this text being read, use two fingers and tap once on the screen to mute the screen reader. Otherwise you will continue to hear this... but maybe you like hearing this? Again, if you ever want to mute VoiceOver when it starts reading text, such as a long article you accidentally selected on a webpage, use two fingers and a single tap to mute it. Hopefully you have muted this by now.

Note: Turning Speech On and Off

You can permanently enable or disable screen reader speech by performing a three-finger double-tap.

Tip: If you have the Zoom feature enabled, you must use a three-finger triple-tap instead. This is because a three-finger double-tap is already reserved as the gesture to toggle the Zoom magnifying tool.
