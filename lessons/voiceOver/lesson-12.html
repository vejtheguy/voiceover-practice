Lesson 12: Notification & Control Center

Similar to the navigation gestures that utilize sound, there is a similar process for opening the Notification or Control Center.

Notification Center

Using one finger, slide down from the top edge of the iPad slowly. Listen closely, as you want to hear three notes play. This opens the Notification Center, where you can navigate and view your current notifications.

Another way to open the Notification Center is to tap on the Status Bar at the top of the screen (where the time or battery percentage is located). Once the Status Bar is selected, perform a three-finger swipe down gesture.

To close the Notification Center, you can use a single-finger slide up from the bottom of the iPad, listening for two notes to play. Alternatively, you can use the Z-gesture (scrub) to return back to your previous screen.

Try opening and closing the Notification Center now using both methods. While it is open, use single-finger left and right swipe gestures to navigate between your different notifications before using the scrub gesture to close it.

Note: Sometimes the Notification Center does not register the Z-gesture (scrub) to close the menu. If this happens, use a single-finger slide up from the bottom edge of the screen instead.

Control Center

Using one finger, slide down from the top edge of the iPad slowly. Listen closely, as you want to hear two notes play. This opens the Control Center panel, which can be customized to include any quick-action features you prefer.

The Control Center can also be opened by selecting the Status Bar at the top of the screen, then performing a three-finger swipe up gesture.

Tip: Use the Z-gesture (scrub) to easily close the Control Center panel and return to your page.

Try opening the Control Center now. Use single-finger left and right swipe gestures to explore your quick settings (like adjusting volume or brightness components). Once you are finished, use the Z-gesture to back out and return to this page.
